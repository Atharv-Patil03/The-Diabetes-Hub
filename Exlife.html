Exercise & Lifestyle for Diabetes Management

Regular physical activity and a healthy lifestyle are powerful tools in managing diabetes. They can help control blood sugar levels, improve cardiovascular health, reduce stress, and enhance overall well-being.

View Exercise Plans

Benefits of Exercise for Diabetes

📉 Blood Sugar Control

Physical activity helps your body use insulin more efficiently, lowering blood glucose levels. Regular exercise can:

  • Increase insulin sensitivity
  • Lower blood glucose during and after activity
  • Reduce A1C levels over time
  • Decrease medication requirements

❤️ Heart Health

People with diabetes have a higher risk of heart disease. Exercise benefits cardiovascular health by:

  • Lowering blood pressure
  • Improving cholesterol levels
  • Strengthening the heart muscle
  • Reducing inflammation

⚖️ Weight Management

Maintaining a healthy weight is crucial for diabetes management. Regular exercise helps:

  • Burn calories and reduce body fat
  • Preserve lean muscle mass
  • Boost metabolism
  • Prevent weight regain

😌 Mental Wellbeing

Living with diabetes can be stressful. Physical activity improves mental health by:

  • Reducing stress and anxiety
  • Enhancing mood and energy levels
  • Improving sleep quality
  • Boosting self-confidence

Weekly Exercise Plan for Type 1 Diabetes

This plan is designed specifically for individuals with Type 1 diabetes. Always check your blood sugar before, during, and after exercise. Adjust insulin and carbohydrate intake as needed.

Day Type of Exercise Duration Intensity Special Considerations
Monday Walking or Light Jogging 30 minutes Moderate Check BG before, during, and after
Tuesday Strength Training (Upper Body) 20-30 minutes Moderate May require less insulin adjustment
Wednesday Rest or Gentle Yoga 20-30 minutes Low Focus on stress reduction
Thursday Cycling or Swimming 30 minutes Moderate Have fast-acting carbs available
Friday Strength Training (Lower Body) 20-30 minutes Moderate Watch for delayed hypoglycemia
Saturday Longer Cardio Session 45-60 minutes Moderate Reduce insulin dose accordingly
Sunday Active Recovery (Walking) 20-30 minutes Low Light activity to aid recovery

Exercise Safety Tips

🩸 Blood Glucose Monitoring

  • Check blood glucose before, during, and after exercise
  • Avoid exercise if glucose is >250 mg/dL with ketones
  • Consider a small snack if glucose is <100 mg/dL
  • Wear CGM or have testing supplies readily available
