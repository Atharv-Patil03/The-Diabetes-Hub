Understanding A1C

A1C (also called HbA1c or glycated hemoglobin) is a blood test that reflects your average blood glucose levels over the past 2-3 months. It shows what percentage of your hemoglobin is coated with sugar (glycated).

For most adults with diabetes, the American Diabetes Association recommends an A1C target of less than 7%. Higher targets may be appropriate for some individuals depending on their specific health conditions.

  • Below 5.7%: Normal
  • 5.7% to 6.4%: Prediabetes
  • 6.5% or higher: Diabetes
