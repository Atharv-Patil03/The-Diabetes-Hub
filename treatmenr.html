Diabetes Treatment Options

Diabetes management requires a comprehensive, personalized approach. Modern treatment options range from lifestyle modifications to advanced medications and technologies. The right treatment plan depends on your diabetes type, health status, preferences, and goals.

This guide provides an overview of available diabetes treatment options, from standard approaches to emerging therapies. Work with your healthcare team to create a tailored plan that best fits your needs.

Medication Options

Various medications help manage diabetes by affecting how your body produces or uses insulin. Each class works differently and may be prescribed alone or in combination.

💊Metformin

First-line medication for type 2 diabetes that reduces glucose production in the liver and improves insulin sensitivity.

💊Sulfonylureas

Stimulate insulin production in the pancreas. Examples include glipizide, glyburide, and glimepiride.

💊DPP-4 Inhibitors

Improve blood glucose control without causing weight gain. Include sitagliptin, saxagliptin, and linagliptin.

Lower blood glucose by preventing kidneys from reabsorbing sugar. Include empagliflozin, dapagliflozin, and canagliflozin.

Insulin Therapy

Insulin therapy is essential for all people with type 1 diabetes and may be needed for some with type 2 diabetes. Several types of insulin are available, each with different onset times and durations.

💉Rapid-Acting Insulin

Begins working within 15 minutes and lasts 2-4 hours. Used at mealtimes. Examples include lispro, aspart, and glulisine.

💉Short-Acting Insulin

Begins working within 30 minutes and lasts 3-6 hours. Regular or R insulin is the most common type.

💉Intermediate-Acting Insulin

Begins working within 2-4 hours and lasts 12-18 hours. NPH insulin is the most common type.

Lifestyle Interventions

Lifestyle modifications form the foundation of diabetes management for all types. These changes can dramatically improve blood glucose control and overall health.

🍎Nutrition Therapy

Individualized meal plans focused on portion control, carb counting, and choosing foods with a low glycemic index.
